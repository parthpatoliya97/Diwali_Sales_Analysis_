Conclusion :-¶
  • Based on the Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) of the Diwali Sales dataset, the following key insights were observed:
Age Group Contribution¶

-The majority of sales were contributed by customers in the 26–35 age group, making them the most dominant segment.

Gender Insights¶

  • Women contributed to a larger share of overall sales compared to men.

  • Within this group, married women were identified as the most significant contributors.

Geographical Trends¶

-The highest sales were recorded in the states of Uttar Pradesh, Maharashtra, and Karnataka, highlighting them as the top-performing regions.

Product Categories¶
  • Most purchases came from Food items, Clothing & Apparel, Electronics, and Footwear, indicating strong demand in these categories.
Occupation Influence¶
  • Customers working in sectors such as IT, Healthcare, and Aviation were the largest contributors to overall sales.
Summary¶
  • In conclusion, married women aged 26–35 years, especially from UP, Maharashtra, and Karnataka, working in IT, Healthcare, and Aviation sectors, and purchasing mainly food, clothing, electronics, and footwear items, formed the primary customer base driving Diwali sales.
